Marine Wildlife Watch Of The Philippines

Watching over marine fauna for over a decade

Organization background
Marine Wildlife Watch of the Philippines (MWWP) is a non-profit, non-stock conservation organization that aims to develop a better appreciation of the marine environment, its ecological processes and how it affects all of us through activities that focus on long-lived marine animals, also referred to as Endangered, Threatened, and Protected Marine Wildlife (ETPs-MW), such as dolphins, whales, dugong, sharks, rays, and marine turtles: iconic species of the marine environment. Marine Wildlife Watch of the Philippines was incorporated on the 13th of September 2010.
What We've Done So Far
The Marine Wildlife Watch of the Philippines has implemented a comprehensive array of strategies for marine conservation. These initiatives involve robust surveillance and monitoring efforts, assessing coral reef health, tracking marine species, and combating illegal fishing practices and poaching. Prioritizing community engagement, the initiatives conduct educational programs to promote awareness of marine biodiversity and advocate for sustainable fishing practices. Collaborative efforts with local communities include the establishment and management of Marine Protected Areas (MPAs) to safeguard critical habitats. Scientific research is integral, providing insights into marine ecosystems, identifying threats, and informing evidence-based conservation strategies. Furthermore, the initiatives engage in the rescue and rehabilitation of injured or stranded marine animals, and collaborate closely with government agencies to enhance policies and regulations for the effective protection of marine wildlife.
